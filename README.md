# Mobile-Surface-Mining-Excavator-TA211

A scaled physical model of a **Mobile Surface Mining Excavator**, inspired by industrial **Bucket Wheel Excavators (BWE)**. The model recreates the mechanical design and working principle of continuous-excavation mining machines: a rotating bucket wheel that digs continuously and feeds material onto a conveyor system, all mounted on a mobile wheeled base.

Built as a course project for **TA211 – Manufacturing Processes I**, Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur.

---

## Overview

Conventional excavators dig in discrete steps — **dig, lift, dump**. Bucket Wheel Excavators instead perform **continuous excavation**: a large rotating wheel fitted with a series of buckets scoops material as it turns and deposits it onto a conveyor belt for transport. This continuous process removes large quantities of soil and minerals in far less time, giving:

- **Higher productivity**
- **Improved operational efficiency**
- **Reduced time consumption**

This project builds a scaled model that highlights the mechanical design of such a machine — its rotating bucket wheel, structural boom, conveyor system, and support linkage — as an educational demonstrator of continuous excavation and material handling.

---

## What Is a Bucket Wheel Excavator?

A Bucket Wheel Excavator (BWE) is a massive piece of heavy equipment used in surface mining and civil engineering, acting as a continuous digging machine in large-scale open-pit operations. Industrial BWEs are among the largest land vehicles ever built — the famous **Bagger 293** stands roughly 96 metres tall and weighs over 14,000 tonnes.

As the bucket wheel spins, its buckets cut into the earth; at the top of the rotation the material falls onto a **discharge boom** and a system of conveyor belts. This lets BWEs move tens of thousands of cubic metres of earth per day. Its key components are the **bucket wheel, main boom, discharge boom, operator's cabin, and crawler assembly** — each of which is represented in this scaled model.

## Objectives

- Recreate the **continuous excavation mechanism** of a BWE at a small scale.
- Demonstrate coordinated operation between the **bucket wheel, boom, conveyor, and mobile base**.
- Apply and integrate a wide range of **manufacturing processes** (shearing, welding, casting, forging, machining, 3D printing).
- Connect theoretical mechanical-design knowledge with practical, hands-on fabrication.

---

## Key Subsystems

| Subsystem | Function |
|---|---|
| **Bucket Wheel** | Rotating wheel fitted with buckets that continuously scoop/dig material. |
| **Excavator Arm / Boom** | Structural arm carrying the bucket wheel and setting the dig geometry. |
| **Conveyor System** | Belt frame with support rollers that transports excavated material away. |
| **Operator's Cabin** | Represents the control station of the machine. |
| **Mobile Wheeled Base** | Base plate, tyres, and axles that make the model mobile (analogous to the crawler). |
| **Support Linkage** | Pins and mounting plates tying the assembly together. |

## How It Was Built

1. **Design** — Each part was modelled to scale and detailed as a dimensioned drawing (see `cad/`).
2. **Fabricate** — Sheet-metal parts were sheared, bent, and welded; rods were swaged; the base plate was plasma-cut; the boom support was forged; the tyres were sand-cast; and the bucket wheel shaft was 3D printed.
3. **Assemble** — Subsystems (bucket wheel, arm, conveyor, cabin, wheeled base) were joined onto the base plate using M3 fasteners in the final main assembly.
